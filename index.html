HISTSCI 119VH StoryMap Project

My project was centered around a reading by James Delbourgo about the history and science of chocolate as related to U.K. physician-botanist Hans Sloane.

This specific map is a German map from 1854 created by cartographer Joseph Meyer.

This is the StoryMap I presented.

“What we often think of narrowly as western scientific objects – herbarium specimens, species illustrations, classification systems and ethnographic objects – should be recognized as artifacts of cross-cultural exchange and contest over landscapes, their uses and their histories”

Reflections on the Project and the Hero Narrative

StoryMap, while an excellent platform to tell a chronological story, is imperfect. Analytical limit.

Map Markers

I drew up custom map markers for this project. This hand-drawn image, shown here, is an artistic rendering inspired heavily by Sloane's etching of cacao, as well as several other botanical drawings of the plant. It appears as the project page favicon as well, to aid readers in distinguishing the project page from other browser tabs.
